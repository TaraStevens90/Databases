The Benefits of Transitioning to a Database System:

Most businesses can immediately benefit from implementing an Online Transaction Processing (OLTP) database, which enables fast, reliable, and real-time handling of everyday transactions. Whether it's recording a sale, updating inventory, or managing customer orders, an OLTP database ensures that data is captured instantly and accurately.

From the moment a customer makes a purchase, the system automatically adjusts stock levels, logs the transaction, and updates your financial records. The result is a streamlined workflow with the added benefit of reduced human error. This keeps your business running smoothly!

What else can a database do for my company?

Regardless of the size of your business, we all have questions that need answers and sometimes quickly. A database system empowers your company with ad hoc querying capabilities, enabling you to ask customized and spontaneous questions about your business. When insights like these are only a few clicks away, productivity increases. Employees can spend less time searching for the information you requested, and you can spend more time acting on it!

Is it really better than using Excel?

Unlike Excel files, which can be duplicated or inconsistently updated, or paper files that can be misplaced or lost entirely, a centralized database helps reduce redundancies and data anomalies by maintaining a single source of truth. A database enforces data consistency through structured formats and validation checks. Let's not forget, when data is inconsistently kept, it's at heightened risk for security breaches. A database improves data security with access controls and encryption, while also offering backups to protect your sensitive business and customer information from loss or unauthorized access.
